Best Noise-Cancelling Headphones for Commuting (2026)

We tested 23 wireless noise-cancelling headphones over 6 weeks of daily commuting on NYC subways and buses. Here are the 4 that made our final cut.

By Daniel Park · Updated Feb 20, 2026 · 12 min read
How we tested We evaluated noise cancellation on the NYC subway (85-95 dB ambient), measured battery life at 50% volume with ANC on, tested comfort over 2-hour sessions, and checked sound quality across genres. Ratings reflect our weighted scoring: ANC performance (40%), value (25%), battery life (20%), comfort (15%).

BassElite X1

Disappointing for the price. At $199, it's the most expensive here but has the weakest ANC (28 dB — we could still hear conversations on the train). 22-hour battery is well below average. The bass-heavy tuning is fun for EDM but muddy for everything else. Heavy at 290g. Hard to recommend when the SoundArc exists.

$199
SoundArc Pro 7

The clear winner. Best-in-class noise cancellation at 45 dB reduction silenced the 6 train almost completely. Exceptional 38-hour battery means weekly charging at most. At $179, it undercuts several competitors with worse specs. Only downside: the carrying case feels cheap for the price.

$179
AudioWave 500

Best value under $120. ANC at 35 dB won't fully block a subway announcement but handles steady train noise well. 30-hour battery is solid. Sound signature leans warm with boosted bass — great for hip-hop and pop, less ideal for classical. Comfortable even with glasses.

$119
ClearTone ANC

A competent mid-ranger that doesn't stand out. 38 dB ANC is respectable and handles most commute noise. 28-hour battery is fine but trails the leaders. Sound is neutral and clean — audiophiles may prefer it — but the plastic build feels less premium than the price suggests.

$159
